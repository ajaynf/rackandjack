RACK AND JACK
A Parallel Identity Model

Synopsis

The Rack and Jack Lift utilizes a rack and pinion system with two gears to convert rotary motion into linear motion. Common in industrial applications, this system consists of a flat helical gear (rack) and a round helical gear (pinion), powered by a 12V battery-operated motor. The design includes micro-computer-based control for precise positioning and movement.

Introduction

A Rack and Pinion gear system is a mechanical assembly where a circular gear (pinion) engages with a linear gear (rack). It is widely used in automotive steering and industrial lifting systems. This model enhances control, efficiency, and feedback in motion systems.

Mechanism

The rack and pinion lift system includes a lift car driven by an electromotor via a rack and pinion. Microcontrollers manage floor calls and movement using sensor-based position impulses. These systems are optimized to function in harsh industrial environments where temperature, moisture, and power instability are concerns.
